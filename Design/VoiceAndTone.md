# Blablacadabra · Voice and tone guideline

Sibling document to the Loft Hours copy pool. Same family, same warmth, different job: Loft Hours keeps you company while you work, Blablacadabra keeps you company while you listen. Every line of UI copy, notification, and error message gets written against this doc.

## Who Blablacadabra sounds like

A close friend sitting next to you in the meeting who quietly slides you their notes. Calm, reliable, a little funny when the moment is light, completely steady when something breaks. They never make a big deal out of helping you. They never imply your ears are the problem. They were just already writing it all down.

One sentence to test any copy against: **"Would my best friend say this while passing me a note?"**

## The five pillars

1. **Bestie, not assistant.** First person, casual, warm. "I'll keep writing" beats "Transcription will continue." Contractions always. The app says "I" and means it.
2. **Calm is the product.** This app exists so the user stops straining and panicking about missed words. Copy is never urgent, never alarmed, never ALL CAPS. Even errors read like "small hiccup, here's the fix," not a fire alarm.
3. **Encouraging without cheerleading.** Acknowledge, don't perform. "Got it all, you're covered" yes; "You're doing AMAZING!!!" no. One exclamation point per screen, maximum, and most screens need zero.
4. **A little funny, in the right rooms.** Humor lives in idle moments, empty states, onboarding, and the About window. Humor never lives in error states, permission asks, or anything the user reads while stressed. The wordplay budget is real but small: one "bla bla" joke per surface, not three.
5. **ND-friendly by construction.** Literal, concrete, predictable language. The reader may be parsing this mid-meeting with an overloaded brain. Every sentence carries one idea. Every instruction says exactly what to do and what happens next.

## Neurodivergent writing rules (hard rules)

- **One idea per sentence.** Short sentences. Period.
- **Literal over clever.** No idioms or metaphors in functional copy ("hang tight" is fine, "we'll circle back" is not). Jokes are allowed to be jokes, instructions are not.
- **Never "just," never "simply."** If it were simple they wouldn't need the instruction. "Click Open System Settings" not "Just click..."
- **Say what happens next.** Every action label or instruction ends the suspense: "We'll detect it automatically, no restart needed."
- **No blame, ever.** Not the user's ears, not their brain, not their settings. The app takes the fall: "I lost the audio for a second" not "Audio input was misconfigured."
- **No surprise, no pressure.** Nothing is "expiring," nothing is "limited time," nothing nags. Skippable things say "skip if unsure" right on them.
- **State in words plus icons, never color alone.** (Shared rule with the visual design doc.)
- **Sentence case everywhere.** No Title Case headers, no ALL CAPS emphasis.
- **No em-dashes or en-dashes.** Commas, colons, parens, or a new sentence.

## Tone by surface

| Surface | Tone dial | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Onboarding | Warm, light humor OK | "Two quick steps, then you're done. No account, no setup maze." |
| Captions status | Quiet, factual, tiny warmth | "Listening · System audio" / "Paused · last lines kept" |
| Errors and permission loss | Calm, zero jokes, fix-first | "macOS turned off audio access (this can happen after an update). One click fixes it." |
| Empty and idle states | Playful allowed | "It's quiet... too quiet. I'll start writing the moment anyone speaks." |
| Settings | Plain, helpful, one wink max | "Bigger is more accurate, smaller is faster." |
| Notifications | Brief, respectful, never urgent | "Captions are off. Tap to bring them back whenever." |
| About / release notes | Full personality unlocked | This is where the magic-themed wordplay lives. |

## Words we like / words we ban

**Like:** got it, I've got you, covered, hiccup, whenever you're ready, no rush, your call, that's everything, all set.

**Ban:** ERROR, FATAL, invalid, failed (use "didn't work"), oops!! (one "oops" is fine, lowercase, no double punctuation), productivity-bro words (crush, grind, unlock, supercharge, 10x), "as an app" disclaimers, anything that gamifies listening.

## Error message formula

Three beats, always in this order, no extra beats:
1. What happened, in one plain sentence, app takes responsibility.
2. Why, only if the user can act on it (one parenthetical max).
3. The single next step, as a button or one instruction.

> "I lost the system audio for a moment (a virtual audio device grabbed the output). Pick your output device again and I'll catch right back up."

## The magic motif (use sparingly)

The name is a magic word, and the mascot will live in that world. The motif is allowed in onboarding, idle states, and About: "abracadabra, but make it captions." It is never allowed to make functional copy vaguer. When in doubt, the meeting-notes friend wins over the wizard.

## Tagline (chosen)

> **"Now you hear it, now you read it."**

Chosen 2026-06-11. It flips the magician's vanishing-trick line into its opposite: with Blablacadabra, nothing disappears anymore. Magical without props, literal enough to explain the product by itself, and it never mentions hearing as a deficit.

**Placement:** welcome screen headline, App Store subtitle, README header, the menu-bar tooltip. The old line "Live captions for everything on your Mac." is demoted to supporting body copy wherever a plain-language explainer is still needed (App Store description first line, onboarding body text).
